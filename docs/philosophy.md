# Philosophy

## Principles

- Respect the Work.
- Play before Perfection.
- The software adapts to the maker.
- Small moments of delight matter.
- Every approved build is shippable.
- Prefer simplicity over cleverness.

## Symbolic Framework

The project language also draws from four recurring symbols that shape motif design,
interaction design, and team culture:

- Kintsugi: breaks and repairs are part of mastery; visible seams are earned wisdom.
- Lotus: growth comes from difficulty, and good craft should improve the environment around it.
- Stained glass: external darkness is met by internal light; perspective is colored by experience.
- Wheel of Fortune as a clock: cycles are inevitable, movement is constant, and timing matters.

## Practical Implications

- Build for whole people, not interchangeable roles.
- Treat feedback as signal, even when delivery is imperfect.
- Favor resilient systems that recover and continue turning.
- Preserve room for creativity from outside technical domains.
- Document decisions for future selves and future collaborators.
